# Econometric Analysis of Salaries in Professional Football

This project was carried out as part of an econometrics study on wage inequalities in professional football.  
Its objective is to analyze the factors explaining salary differences among the 463 highest-valued outfield players in September 2024.

## 📑 Project Overview
The study focuses on outfield players, meaning all players except goalkeepers, including defenders, midfielders, and forwards.

We collected **performance**, **sociodemographic**, and **contractual** data for the **2023/2024 season**.  
After cleaning the dataset, the final sample included **463 players**.

The project combines:
- a **descriptive analysis** of the dataset,
- and an **econometric analysis** to identify the main determinants of player salaries.

## 📌 Project Objectives
- 📊 Analyze salary disparities among professional football players
- 📈 Identify the variables that significantly influence wages
- 🔍 Study the impact of sporting performance, individual characteristics, popularity, and club affiliation on salaries

## 📂 Project Structure
- 📁 `data/` : Contains the dataset used for the analysis
- 📁 `scripts/` : R scripts for data cleaning, descriptive statistics, and econometric modeling
- 📁 `results/` : Outputs, tables, and results of the analyses

## 🧾 Main Findings
The study shows that salary differences are not explained only by expected sporting performance.  
Other factors also play an important role, such as:
- individual characteristics,
- player popularity,
- and the club to which the player belongs.
